Chemical methods involve the use of reagents like alcohol, phenol, or chaotropic salts to precipitate and purify DNA. In contrast, biotechnology methods utilize innovative techniques such as magnetic bead-based purification and column chromatography that enhance efficiency and purity. Both approaches are pivotal in research, clinical diagnostics, and forensic applications, driven by advances in technology and increasing demand for genetic analysis.</p></p>
