<p><p>ICAD, or Intracranial Atherosclerotic Disease, involves the narrowing of arteries in the brain due to plaque buildup, leading to increased stroke risk. The market for ICAD treatment includes stents and PTA (Percutaneous Transluminal Angioplasty) balloons. Stents are deployed to keep narrowed arteries open, providing support and preventing re-narrowing. PTA balloons, on the other hand, are used to dilate the affected artery. Together, these interventions aim to restore blood flow and improve outcomes for patients with ICAD.</p></p>
