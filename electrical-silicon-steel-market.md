<p><p>The electrical silicon steel market is categorized into low silicon and high silicon types based on silicon content, which enhances magnetic properties. Low silicon steel, containing about 0.5% to 3% silicon, is commonly used in transformers and electrical machines, offering improved efficiency and reduced energy losses. High silicon steel, with silicon content around 3% to 6%, provides superior magnetic characteristics and is primarily utilized in high-frequency applications like electric motors and generators, where enhanced performance is critical.</p></p>
