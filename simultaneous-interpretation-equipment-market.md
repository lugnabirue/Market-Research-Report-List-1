<p><p>The simultaneous interpretation equipment market is divided into two primary types: cable and wireless systems. Cable-based systems use physical connections to transmit audio, providing reliable sound quality and eliminating interference but limiting mobility. In contrast, wireless systems utilize radio frequencies, offering greater flexibility and freedom of movement for interpreters and listeners. While wireless technology enhances accessibility and is preferred for larger venues, cable systems are often favored in settings where stability and low latency are critical.</p></p>
